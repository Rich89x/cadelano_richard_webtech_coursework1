The Caesar Cipher

The Caesar Cipher, named after Julius Caesar, is one of the most anciest ciphers known,
it consists into shift each letter of the message by an agreed number, common use in the past
was 3 positions, to encrypt the message. It is though that Julius Caesar developed this cipher
to transmit secret messages to his generals.

Beside the simplicity of use of this cipher, is necessary to consider that it is an higly
unsecure encryption method, that could be easily brake by hands. There is a variable of the
caesar cipher called ROT13 where the letters are switched with a fixed number of 13 positions.
