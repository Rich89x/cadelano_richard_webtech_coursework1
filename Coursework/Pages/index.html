Ciphers and Encryption

Cryptohraphy is a method to secure communications by tranlating a "plain text" message
into an undecryptable message. Is a method used since the ancient times to protect secret
conversations and provide security.
The fist known example of Cryptohraphy is represented by the use of hyeroglyphics in the Ancient Egypt,
where the "original message" was translated into that kind of text by scribes.

Even if in this particular case we are not talking about a secret writing, that could be considered
as the first example of a message translation.

Retracing the history of Cryptohraphy, is almost impossible to not mention Julius Caesar and his
Caesar Shift Cipher: in this cipher the letters of the message are shifted by an agreed number of
positions, so when the message arrived at destination, the recipied had to shift the letters back to
obtain the original text.

Another famous example of Cryptografy is represented by the Morse Code: this code consist into translate
each letter of the message into a series of short signals (represented as Dots) and long signals (represented as dashes).
